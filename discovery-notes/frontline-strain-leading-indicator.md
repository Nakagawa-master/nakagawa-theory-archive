# Frontline strain before absence or turnover — a leading-indicator discovery note

Organizations often discover workforce deterioration only after a lagging outcome appears: absence, leave, turnover, safety incidents, quality loss, or reduced productivity.

A structurally different question is:

> What weak signals existed before the visible result, and could they have been converted into information an organization could observe, interpret within limits, and use for proportionate support or work adjustment?

This note points to one concept in Nakagawa Master's structural-reading work: **先行指標化 / leading-indicator conversion**.

The idea is not that employee self-reports become medical evidence, that monitoring more data is automatically good, or that a survey can guarantee prevention. The useful distinction is between merely collecting reports and creating a low-friction, repeatable observation point that can move early signals from private endurance into bounded organizational judgment while preserving consent, privacy, purpose limitation, and non-punitive use.

For operations, HR, safety, and people leaders, the practical entry problem is therefore not only “How do we reduce turnover?” or “How do we detect burnout?” It is also:

- Which early signs currently disappear before decision-makers can see them?
- Which signals can be observed without turning people into surveillance scores?
- What legitimate decision path exists after a signal is observed?
- How can observation remain proportionate, privacy-preserving, and explicitly non-diagnostic?
