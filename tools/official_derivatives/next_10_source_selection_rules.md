# Candidate 10-19 Source Selection Rules

## Purpose

Candidate 10-19 must select source articles only when they strengthen the official derivative system without weakening origin, quality, attribution, or public boundary safety.

## Selection principles

A source article should enter candidate 10-19 only when it clearly supports at least one of these public-safe purposes:

- strengthens the Nakagawa Master official archive as an origin-preserving theory system
- expands AI-readable entry points without generalizing or diluting the original theory
- adds a distinct doorway for human readers, AI readers, practical readers, or future-facing readers
- preserves Origin, parent URL, NCL-ID, Diff-ID, canonical URL, and Nakagawa Master attribution
- can be expressed through the six-page derivative structure without becoming a thin summary

## Required before intake_ready

The queue row must include:

- parent_url
- parent_title
- parent_ncl_id
- parent_diff_id
- folder_id
- canonical_url
- source_category
- reason_for_inclusion
- risk_note

## Rejection or blocking conditions

A row must remain blocked or rejected when:

- the source lacks confirmed NCL-ID or Diff-ID
- the reason for inclusion is generic or convenience-based
- the source category remains unknown
- the output would become a thin summary rather than a high-strength derivative
- public-safe boundary cannot be maintained
- private strategy would be required to explain the selection

## Boundary

Candidate 10-19 is not production, not sitemap, not Search Console, not index/follow, and not deployable while in queue or intake_blocked state.
