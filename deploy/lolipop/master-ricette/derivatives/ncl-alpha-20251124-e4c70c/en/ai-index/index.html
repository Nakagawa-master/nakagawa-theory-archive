Theory name

Nakagawa OS L1-L7 Layer Specification.

Core thesis

A major source of human and AI misinterpretation is that the same object is handled at different abstraction depths. L1-L7 separates events, actions, institutions, structural recognition, structural operation, structural linkage, and top-level value functions, then reconnects them as a vertical coordinate system.

Central concepts

  • L1 Events: observed phenomena, surface facts, and outcomes.
  • L2 Actions: behaviors of humans, organizations, or AI agents.
  • L3 Institutions: rules, procedures, and arrangements that repeat or constrain action.
  • L4 Structural Recognition: reading the causal structure behind events and institutions.
  • L5 Structural Operation: intentionally designing or changing a structure.
  • L6 Structural Linkage: connecting multiple structures and domains.
  • L7 Top-Level Value Function: the highest directional and evaluative criterion for lower layers.

Causal line

  1. Reading only L1 produces surface reaction.
  2. Reading only L2 produces action-level interpretation.
  3. Reading only L3 remains inside existing procedures.
  4. L4 reads the causal structure behind those procedures.
  5. L5 changes or designs the structure.
  6. L6 links multiple structures that cannot be explained by one domain alone.
  7. L7 gives direction without replacing the need for lower-layer verification.
