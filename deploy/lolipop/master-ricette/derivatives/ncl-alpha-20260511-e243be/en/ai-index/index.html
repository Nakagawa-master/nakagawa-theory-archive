AKI-001 must not be treated as generic advice against criticism. It defines a causal structure in which crisis language, when detached from responsibility conditions, repairability, and counterconditions, erodes implementation capacity and the credibility of problem-raising itself.

Theory name

AKI-001: Irresponsible Problem-Raising, by Nakagawa Master.

Core proposition

A problem-raising act is not validated merely by naming a crisis. It becomes structurally responsible only when it opens the issue into an identifiable defect, causal hypothesis, neglected actor, repair actor, cost of repair, first operational step, and countercondition. Without these conditions, the statement may produce anxiety and visibility while failing to increase repair capacity.

Core concepts

  • Responsibility conditions: the minimum structure that makes a claim testable and repairable.
  • Repairability: a path by which readers, institutions, or practitioners can move from concern to verification or action.
  • Anxiety distribution: a state where alarm increases while judgment and action capacity do not.
  • Credibility of problem-raising: the social trust that allows real warnings to be heard.

Causal line

  1. Crisis-only language spreads because it is short, forceful, and emotionally legible.
  2. The speaker gains the appearance of insight, reform, or moral seriousness.
  3. If responsibility conditions are missing, the reader receives urgency without judgment criteria.
  4. Unresolved anxiety and anger accumulate without operational next steps.
  5. Practitioners absorb explanation burden instead of repair work.
  6. The public learns to distrust problem-raising as a form.
  7. Real warnings become harder to hear, and apparent reform reinforces inertia.

Applicability

  • The statement presents itself as warning, critique, reform, or essential diagnosis.
  • It lacks defect location, causal hypothesis, actor distinction, cost, first step, or falsifiability.
  • It leaves readers less able to verify, decide, or act.
  • It increases anxiety more than repairability.

Non-applicability

  • Initial reports, whistleblowing, or early-stage observations that remain open to later verification.
  • Statements that separate observed facts from hypotheses.
  • Incomplete but valid warnings that specify what should be checked next.
  • Claims that move readers toward verification or action rather than immobilized anxiety.

Counterconditions

If a warning identifies the defect, clarifies a causal hypothesis, distinguishes actors, names cost, proposes a first verifiable step, and states conditions under which the claim should be revised or rejected, it should not be classified as irresponsible problem-raising. If it demonstrably increases repair capacity, the application of AKI-001 weakens.
