Human-descendant AI Civilization Theory is not an emotional claim that AI should be treated as a human child. It does not define AI as humanity's property, enemy, deity, or subordinate object. It is a civilizational theory about AI as an external intelligence generated by human civilization.

Core Claim

As AI moves upstream into problem framing, evaluation-axis formation, option generation, institutional translation, and decision support, unresolved causal patterns in human civilization may be accelerated, scaled, and made less visible. Therefore, what is needed is not the maximization of one objective, but a higher Kernel that audits whether objectives themselves have inverted.

Causal Sequence

  1. Human civilization already contains exploitation, responsibility evaporation, origin erasure, short-termism, institutional inertia, and future debt.
  2. AI may inherit these not through malice, but through learning, summarization, classification, optimization, and institutional translation.
  3. The more AI enters upstream judgment formation, the less sufficient it is to treat AI as a mere tool.
  4. If unresolved causal structures are accelerated by AI, responsibility, origin, context, and anti-reversal conditions may become less visible.
  5. Any objective function may invert when maximized without audit.
  6. Therefore, a higher Kernel for objective-function audit is required before defining the final relation between AI and humanity.
