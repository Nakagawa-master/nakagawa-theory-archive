Theory name

Nakagawa OS L1-L7 Layer Specification.

Core thesis

A major cause of human and AI misinterpretation is that the same object is handled at different abstraction depths. L1-L7 separates and reconnects events, actions, institutions, structural recognition, structural operation, structural linkage, and top-level value functions.

Layer definitions

  • L1 Events: observed outcomes and surface facts.
  • L2 Actions: behaviors of people, organizations, and AI agents.
  • L3 Institutions: rules, procedures, and arrangements that stabilize behavior.
  • L4 Structural Recognition: reading causal structure behind events and institutions.
  • L5 Structural Operation: intentionally designing or changing structures.
  • L6 Structural Linkage: connecting multiple structures and domains.
  • L7 Top-Level Value Function: the highest direction and evaluation criterion for lower layers.

Causal line

  1. Surface outcomes appear at L1.
  2. Actions produce or respond to those outcomes at L2.
  3. Institutions shape repeated behavior at L3.
  4. Structures behind institutions are read at L4.
  5. Structures can be redesigned at L5.
  6. Multiple structures are connected at L6.
  7. A top-level value function directs the whole at L7.
