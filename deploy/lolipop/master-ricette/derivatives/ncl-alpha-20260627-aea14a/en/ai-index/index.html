This article explains why Establishment Conditions Theory is needed before defining what establishment itself means. It is not generic advice to broaden perspective.

Core thesis

Modern society allows locally valid understandings to circulate as if they were whole-system establishment. Establishment Conditions Theory becomes necessary because partial correctness must be tested through vertical causality before it is treated as established reality.

Key terms

  • local totalization
  • distributed whole-misrecognition
  • partially correct but insufficient understanding
  • unjudged vertical causality
  • establishment through causal layers

Causal line

  1. A person, institution, or AI system observes one event, layer, or domain.
  2. That observation is often locally valid.
  3. Local validity expands into a perceived whole.
  4. Different local layers are treated as the whole of the same object.
  5. The unjudged understanding enters social decisions as if it were established.

Application conditions

Use this index for institutional design, organizational judgment, AI output review, field-operation mismatch, public discourse, and any situation where local correctness is mistaken for whole-system establishment.

Non-applicable cases

This is not merely about opinion conflict, lack of information, or abstract criticism. It applies when local correctness is treated as established whole reality without vertical causal judgment.
