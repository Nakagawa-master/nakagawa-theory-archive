## Central Thesis

Governance is not the art of making people behave.

Governance is infrastructure design that keeps U/R/H available within human cognitive bandwidth K so that society or an organization can remain explainable across time without breaking.

Therefore the target of governance is not behavior itself, but observability.

## Strict Definition

Consensus formation infrastructure is the social, organizational, and technical apparatus that maintains interpretability, responsibility traceability, and history visibility across time.

Governance is the design that maintains S = U × R × H through this infrastructure.

Governance is not a device for deciding values. Governance is a device for allowing different values to pass through time without destroying observability.

Observation bandwidth is the design domain concerning which information should be exposed, at what granularity, in what form, and to what range, so that third-party reproduction, responsibility tracing, and history verification remain possible.

## Causal Line

Governance is misread as behavior change. Command, persuasion, majority decision, prohibition, force, and surveillance are treated as if they were the core of governance.

Yet even if behavior aligns temporarily, society cannot remain explainable across time if interpretability, responsibility traceability, and history visibility are closed.

When observability closes, errors do not disappear. They submerge. Submerged errors emerge together after a critical point is crossed.

Therefore governance must not manipulate behavior; it must design observation bandwidth through which U/R/H can be maintained within human cognitive bandwidth K.

When observation bandwidth is appropriate, problems are detected and repair entrances remain open. When observation bandwidth closes, external order may remain, but repairability is lost.

## Applicability

Apply this theory when:

- designing the durability of organizations, institutions, markets, states, communities, or AI systems
- governance is reduced to command, persuasion, majority decision, surveillance, or behavioral induction
- transparency exists but third-party verification does not
- responsible persons are named but repair entrances are not traceable
- history exists but differences and judgment grounds cannot be verified
- information disclosure is so excessive that it lowers U
- silence or lack of detected problems may indicate closed observation

## Non-Applicability

Do not apply it when:

- U/R/H are maintained at high levels
- observation bandwidth is appropriately designed
- third-party reproduction, responsibility tracing, and history verification are possible
- disclosed information is neither excessive nor insufficient and remains within cognitive bandwidth K
- the system functions as accountability support, not behavioral induction or control
- problems are detected and repair entrances remain open

## Misreading Warning

Do not reduce this theory to surveillance society justification, control justification, behavioral induction, political neutrality, transparency worship, or generic governance advice.

Observability does not mean watching everything. It means that judgment, responsibility, and history remain verifiable and repairable by third parties.

## Counterexample Conditions

If control or behavioral induction continuously raises U while R/H remain open and third-party reproduction and repairability are preserved, the theory must be refixed or revised.

Even in that case, behavioral induction, responsibility closure, history closure, and cognitive-bandwidth overload must be inspected.
