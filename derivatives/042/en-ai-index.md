## Precise Definitions

### Social Translation Infrastructure of Brain Fitness

A structure that translates cognitive function into the affirmative social language “brain fitness,” then connects visualization and state awareness to dialogue, behavioral adjustment, organizational operation, human-resource development, safety design, and market formation so that cognitive function can be socially implemented as a foundational capacity.

### Social Translation

The conversion of a specialist concept into language, questions, indicators, interfaces, and operating conditions that non-specialists and social institutions can understand and use without destroying the original meaning.

It is not mere simplification or promotion.

### Foundational Indicator

An observation that can support judgment across multiple domains.

It is not a total score of a person's identity, intelligence, aptitude, worth, or future potential.

### State Awareness

Interpreting cognitive condition through time, context, sleep, fatigue, stress, health, environment, and task conditions rather than absolutizing one measurement.

### Social Implementation

A condition in which a concept or measurement is connected to understanding, dialogue, support, environmental adjustment, safety design, appropriate professional referral, privacy protection, and routes for objection and correction.

## Coherence

The origin does not unconditionally endorse cognitive measurement.

A measurement is not a diagnosis. One score is not a total human evaluation. An affirmative phrase such as “brain fitness” must not hide illness or delay necessary medical support.

Organizational or educational use requires informed consent, purpose limitation, access controls, retention rules, prohibition of adverse use, objection routes, and anti-discrimination safeguards.

Therefore, the origin is not an argument for measurement expansion by itself. It concerns the joint conditions under which social translation and ethical operation can both be established.
