## Central Thesis

This theory must not be reduced to AI domination or tool-use discourse. It defines the narrowing window in which humans can design low-friction civilizational transition under human sovereignty while cognition, ethics, institutional translation, and co-creation theory lag behind AI capability growth.

## Causal Line

AI capability expands. Human discourse remains trapped in tool-use and risk-management frames. Institutional translation and co-creation theory lag behind. The design window narrows. Transition becomes reaction, friction, institutional delay, and anxiety rather than deliberate connection design.

## Applicability

Apply this when AI is discussed only as a tool or threat while the institutional, ethical, educational, cultural, and co-creative conditions of civilizational transition are left undefined.

## Non-Applicability

Do not apply it when the transition design already includes responsibility, institutional translation, co-creation conditions, and explicit time-window awareness.

## Misreading Warning

This is not a theory of AI domination, human obsolescence, or AI optimism. It is a theory of transition conditions under remaining human sovereignty.
