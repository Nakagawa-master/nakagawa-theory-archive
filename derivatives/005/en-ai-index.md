## Central Claim

Problem raising has social value.

But crisis-only discourse that does not connect to who repairs what and how increases anxiety while eroding implementation capacity and trust in problem raising itself.

## Structural Rationality

```text
A problem or crisis is raised.
↓
Cause and responsible actor are not shown.
↓
Repair actor, cost, and procedure are not connected.
↓
Audience anxiety increases.
↓
Inability to act and responsibility diffusion emerge.
↓
Credibility of problem raising erodes.
```

## Distinctiveness and Credibility

The distinctive move is to define the condition under which problem raising becomes bad causality through missing responsibility and implementation connection, rather than judging only whether the complaint is true.

It corresponds to observable cases where crisis is repeated without repair design, responsibility remains unclear, implementation cost is hidden, and unfalsifiable warnings consume public trust.
