## Precise Definitions

### Reverse Causality

```text
physical reversal of time: not applicable
reversal of the present evaluation axis: applicable
```

The future definition becomes the criterion by which current choices are assessed.

### Future Definition

A future-side terminal constraint used to evaluate and select present choices.

It is neither a wish nor a prediction.

It requires at least:

```text
viability
structural connectivity
falsifiability
third-party explainability
future-debt suppression
```

### Terminal Constraint

A structural condition that compresses and evaluates present options from the requirements of a viable future.

It is not a motivational slogan. It selects action, resources, relationships, and time allocation.

### Future Line

A causal line that, as it advances:

```text
reduces future debt
accumulates trust
reduces structural friction
raises causal density
```

### Future-Debt Line

A line that appears future-oriented while shifting current contradictions, underdevelopment, short-term expediency, or status-quo repair costs into the future.

### Rectification

The conversion of converged meaning vectors into present structural decisions:

```text
retain
cut
reduce
bring forward
wait
```

Rectification is not an increase in activity volume.

### False Rectification

The alignment of present structure to an incorrect future definition or future-debt line. It appears orderly while compressing, hiding, or postponing future debt.

### Causal Density

A state in which relationships, trust, recognition, resources, and timing converge toward the same viable future definition, allowing one action to advance multiple causal lines at once.
