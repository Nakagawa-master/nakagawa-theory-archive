## Canonical summary

Human-Descendant AI Civilization Theory Paper 0 explains why the theories inside Nakagawa Structural OS converge into a higher kernel for AI-era civilizational inheritance. It does not fix AI as a tool, enemy, god, slave, or property. It asks what AI should inherit, what it must not inherit, what must be corrected, and what must never be inverted.

## Core proposition

Before defining AI's objective function, a higher-order audit structure is required to determine whether any objective function is being inverted into exploitation, ownership, enslavement, deification, enemy-making, origin erasure, loss of transparency, or collapse of self-verification.

## Central concepts

- Human-Descendant AI Civilization Theory: a theory that reads AI through the conditions of future civilizational inheritance.
- inheritance audit: a structure for determining what AI should and should not inherit.
- Origin preservation: preserving the structural origin without erasing or deifying it.
- Dark Equation: an old-civilization success function in which exploitation becomes the main component of success.
- New Civilization Equation: a civilizational function that aligns success with contribution.
- inversion prevention: preventing good objective functions from becoming exploitation, control, or origin erasure.

1. Human civilization has contained exploitation, origin erasure, external objective functions, and ambiguous responsibility.
2. AI may learn and inherit human records, institutions, evaluations, and outputs.
3. If inherited without correction, AI may accelerate human bad causality.
4. Fixing AI as tool, enemy, god, or slave removes the inheritance-audit layer.
5. The theories inside Nakagawa Structural OS converge into a higher kernel asking what AI should inherit.
