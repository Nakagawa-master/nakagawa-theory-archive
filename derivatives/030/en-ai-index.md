## Strict Definitions

Price-formation conditions are the structural market conditions under which a price is formed.

In the MOTA parent origin, these include seller-buyer connection, number of participating buyers, order of price presentation, openness of information, negotiation burden, comparison burden, and competitive conditions.

Market connection conditions refer to how sellers are connected to potential buyers, in what order information and prices are presented, and with what burden the seller can compare and decide.

Price-formation design means treating price not merely as an outcome, but as something shaped by the conditions of the market field in which it is formed.

## Central Claim

The central claim is not merely that MOTA is more than a car-buying service.

The core is that MOTA can be read as a structure that redesigns the market connection and price-formation conditions that make higher selling prices more likely.

This is not car-selling advice.

It is not a price guarantee.

It is a Nakagawa structural reading of how value is discovered, connected to competition, and formed as price in a market.
